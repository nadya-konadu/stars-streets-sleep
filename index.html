Linking Nighttime Light in Mississauga to Dream Emotion

Dreaming is a universal human experience.

Across a lifetime, people spend about six years dreaming.

Yet while sleep has been widely studied, far less is known about how our surroundings shape what we dream and how those dreams feel.

Nighttime Light in Mississauga

January

One surrounding factor is light.

In many modern cities, streetlights, buildings, and illuminated infrastructure brighten the night. This artificial brightness becomes a consistent part of the nighttime environment after dark.

The map shows how bright the night is in Mississauga across the year 2025.

Connecting Nighttime Light and Dream

To explore how the nighttime environment relates to dreaming, this project draws on self-reported dream descriptions from The Dream Drop collected through 2025 in Mississauga. These reports capture not only what people dream about, but also the emotional tone of those dreams.
