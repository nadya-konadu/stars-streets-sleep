Linking Nighttime Light in Mississauga to Dream Emotion

Dreaming is a universal human experience.

Across a lifetime, people spend about six years dreaming.

Yet while sleep has been widely studied, far less is known about how our surroundings shape what we dream and how those dreams feel.

Data Sources & Limitations

This project combines environmental satellite data with self-reported dream records to explore possible relationships between nighttime light and dream emotion.

Nighttime light measurements were derived from monthly average radiance composites from the VIIRS Day/Night Band (DNB) satellite product.

Dream data were drawn from The Dream Drop .

These patterns are exploratory and should not be interpreted as causal.
